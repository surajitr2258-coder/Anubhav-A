About Anubhav

An Initiative To Keep Humanity Alive

Who We Are

Anubhav (Registration No.: S0028919) is a registered non-governmental organization based in Diamond Harbour, South 24 Parganas, West Bengal. Founded with the motto "An Initiative To Keep Humanity Alive," we are committed to creating sustainable change through healthcare initiatives, educational empowerment, and environmental conservation.

Officially Registered NGO - Registration No.: S0028919

Our Mission

To keep humanity alive by serving the underprivileged communities across West Bengal. We believe in creating a society where every individual, regardless of their economic background, has access to basic healthcare, quality education, and lives in a clean environment.

"An Initiative To Keep Humanity Alive" - This is not just our tagline, but our promise to every person we serve.

Our Vision

To build healthier, educated, and environmentally conscious communities throughout West Bengal. We envision a state where humanity thrives through collective effort, compassion, and sustainable development.

Our Three Core Projects

Keeping Humanity Alive Through Action

Healthcare Initiative

Serving 2000+ Patients

Providing free medical camps, health awareness programs, and basic healthcare access to underserved communities across South 24 Parganas and neighboring districts.

  • Regular health camps
  • Free medicine distribution
  • Health awareness workshops
  • Emergency medical support
2,000+ Patients Treated
10 Health Camps

Educational Empowerment

Supporting 300+ Students

Bridging educational gaps through free tutoring, scholarship programs, and study material distribution for underprivileged children in West Bengal.

  • Free tutoring classes
  • Study material distribution
  • Career counseling
  • Merit scholarships
300+ Students Enrolled
3 Learning Centers

Environmental Conservation

Greening West Bengal

Leading environmental conservation efforts through tree plantation drives, waste management programs, and awareness campaigns in coastal areas of West Bengal.

  • Tree plantation drives
  • Coastal area cleanup
  • Plastic-free campaigns
  • Eco-awareness programs
2,700+ Beneficiaries
Multiple Green Drives
